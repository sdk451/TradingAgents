They see smartphone markets maturing, margins slipping (down from 26.4 percent), and fierce competition from lower-cost rivals eroding share—especially among younger, less loyal buyers. Finally, they stress that Apple’s premium valuation leaves little room for error; any earnings hiccup could trigger sharp sell-offs in an already volatile tech sector.

I believe the bull case is stronger here. Apple’s service business now represents about 20 percent of revenue, cushioning hardware swings, and its cash flows give it optionality to weather a slowdown. Yes, markets are choppy and growth is harder in saturated segments, but that’s priced in when you look at how resilient Apple has been in past downturns. The risk of sitting on the sidelines outweighs the risk of owning a proven cash-generating machine that’s still innovating and returning capital to shareholders.
